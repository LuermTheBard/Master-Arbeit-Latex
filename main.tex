\begin{center}
	{\Large\bfseries Abstract}
\end{center}
\vspace{0.5cm}
\begin{spacing}{1.0}
\noindent NGC 4593 is a nearby Seyfert I Galaxy exhibiting strong variability from the X-ray to the optical bands \parencite{mchardy2017ngc4593, cackett2018accretion} and prominent broad emission-lines \parencite{kollatschny1997balmer, cackett2018accretion, Ochmann_2025}. In 2016, the galaxy was monitored with HST/STIS (PI: E. M. Cackett, Program ID 14121) from 12 July to 6 August, spanning wavelength ranges of approximately $1100\,\AA$--$1700\,\AA$ and $2900\,\AA$--$10.000\,\AA$. \\
\textit{Aims:} This thesis aims to perform a reverberation-mapping analysis of the broad-line region (BLR) of NGC\,4593 based on the 2016 HST/STIS campaign, to examine its structure, to estimate the mass of the central supermassive black hole (SMBH), and to investigate the variability of the low-ionization line O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda 8446$, which has not previously been monitored in a reverberation-mapping campaign.\\
\textit{Methods:} Based on the HST/STIS spectra of the 26 epochs, a mean and root-mean-square spectrum were derived. Small wavelength misalignment in the optical spectra between epochs was identified and corrected by intercalibration anchored to the narrow [O\,\textsc{iii}]\,$\lambda5007$ line. Continua and emission-line light curves were obtained and cross-correlated with the photometric \textit{Swift} UVW2 light curve presented by \cite{mchardy2018x} using the interpolated cross-correlation function (ICCF). The time lags between the emission-line and UVW2 light curve were determined by deriving the centroid of the top $20\%$ of the cross-correlation function with uncertainties estimated with FR/RSS Monte Carlo simulations. Line widths were measured taking the FWHM from the rms profiles and the SMBH mass was estimated based on the virial mass of the BLR.\\
\textit{Results:} This thesis presents the first reverberation-mapping analysis of the BLR in NGC\,4593 based on an HST/STIS monitoring campaign. The estimated characteristic BLR radii and line widths of the emission-lines are consistent with an ionization stratification structure of the BLR. Based on the reverberation-mapped lines, an SMBH mass of $\bar{M}_{\mathrm{BH}} \approx (5.28 \pm 0.12)\times 10^{7}\,M_\odot$ was derived, which is in good agreement in order of magnitude with the estimates of earlier studies (e.g. \cite{kollatschny1997balmer, denney2006ngc4593}). In addition, variability of the low-ionization line O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda 8446$ was detected for the first time in a reverberation-mapping campaign. This variability was inferred to be primarily driven by Ly$\beta$ fluorescence, allowing to examine the Bowen fluorescence process for the first in a reverberation mapping analysis.
\end{spacing}
