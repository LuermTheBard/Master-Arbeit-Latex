\chapter{Discussion}

In a recent study, \cite{cackett2018accretion} conducted an observing campaign of NGC\,4593 with HST/STIS (PI: Cackett, E.M, Prop ID 14121), covering the optical to NIR between approximately $2900\,\AA$ -- $10000\,\AA$ as well as the UV range between approximately $\sim 1100\,\AA$–$1700\,\AA$. The study focused on a reverberation mapping analysis of the UV and optical continua emission of NGC\,4593 \parencite{cackett2018accretion}. However, a reverberation mapping analysis of the BLR based on this dataset had not yet been attempted. Building on the dataset of \cite{cackett2018accretion}, this thesis presents the first reverberation-mapping analysis of the BLR of NGC\,4593 based on HST/STIS observations, including broad emission-lines that have not been monitored in a reverberation mapping campaign of NGC\,4593 yet. In addition, the HST/STIS dataset showed variability in the low-ionization line O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ line (see Table \ref{tab:varstatistics_lines}), which was never monitored in a reverberation-mapping campaign before. Therefor it was possible to include O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ in the reverberation-mapping analysis, which enabled the first reverberation-mapping analysis of a Bowen-fluorescence line.
 
\section{Structure of the BLR}
\subsection{Time Lags}
The extracted light curves of the emission-lines showed an overall similar shape, with strong correlation with the UVW2 light curve and CCF peak values between $0.7$--$0.9$. Only the O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ light curve showed a lower peak correlation with UVW2 of $0.6$, since recombination was excluded as a significant excitation mechanism for O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$, which will be further discussed in Section \ref{sec:diskussion_BF}.\\
H$\alpha$ and H$\beta$ showed different time lags of $3.3^{+1.1}_{-0.6}\,$ and $2.3^{+1.0}_{-0.3}\,$days which are in good agreement with the results of earlier works \parencite{kollatschny1997balmer, denney2006ngc4593, Williams_2018}. The higher-order Balmer lines H$\gamma$ and H$\delta$ show a significantly shorter time lags with $1.7^{+0.2}_{-0.4}\,$days and $1.7^{+0.4}_{-0.4}\,$days, respectively. This implies radially stratified structure of the Balmer-line emitting region, where higher-order lines respond more rapidly to continuum variations than lower-order lines, consistent with optical depth effects observed for the Balmer-line emitting region \parencite{korista2004optical} \\ 
Looking at the helium lines, they show different time lags depending on their ionization. He\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda5876$ lags $3.3^{+0.7}_{-0.4}\,$days behind UVW2, while the higher-ionized He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda1640$ and He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda4686$ exhibit a much faster response times, with lags of $0.3^{+0.6}_{-0.2}\,$days and $0.7^{+0.1}_{-0.3}\,$days, respectively. Therefore the time lag of He\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda5876$ is consistent with the lag of H$\alpha$, while the He\,\textsc{ii} lines exhibits significantly shorter delays.\\
The high-ionized UV lines all show similar lags within uncertainties, with measured values of $1.0 \ensuremath{_{-0.4}^{+0.2}}$, $1.2 \ensuremath{_{-0.4}^{+0.1}}$ and $0.8 \ensuremath{_{-0.1}^{+0.5}}$ days for N\,\textsc{v}$\,\lambda\lambda 1238,\,1242$, Si\,\textsc{iv}$\,\lambda\lambda 1393,\,1402$ and C\textsc{iv}$\,\lambda\lambda 1548,\,1550$, respectively.\\ The ionization dependency seen for the helium line time lags and the consistent smaller time lags of high-ionization lines compared to the the lower ionized Balmer lines implies an ionization stratification structure of the BLR. This is consistent with BLR structures reported for other Seyfert galaxies (e.g \cite{collin1988broad, gaskell1986line, kollatschny2001short}).\\
Since UVW2 lags behind the extracted HST/STIS $1150\,\AA$ continuum by approximately $0.5\,$days, this offset was added for the final estimation of the characteristic BLR radii for each emission-line (see Table \ref{tab:lags_UVW2}).

\subsection{Line Profiles}
Based on the assumption that the velocity of the BLR is virialized and dominated by the gravitational potential of the SMBH, the velocity dispersion of the BLR can be parameterized by the line widths of the rms line profiles broad emission-line \parencite{peterson2004} and is common in reverberation mapping campaigns(e.g. \cite{kollatschny1997balmer, denney2006ngc4593})\\
The Balmer rms line profiles show a similar asymmetric, double-peaked structure and are shifted toward positive velocities. This shift is also noticeable in the Ly$\alpha$ profile and He\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda5876$ profile. This indicates that most of the line variability of these lines originates from the receding part of the emitting region.\\
Opposing to that, the maxima of the higher-ionization He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda1640$ and He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda4686$ profiles show no velocity shift. A similar behaviour is observed in the rms profiles of the high-ionized UV lines N\,\textsc{v}$\,\lambda\lambda 1238,\,1242$, Si\,\textsc{iv}$\,\lambda\lambda 1393,\,1402$ and C\textsc{iv}$\,\lambda\lambda 1548,\,1550$. High-ionized broad emission-lines often show a blue-shift compared to lower-ionized broad emission-lines \parencite{gaskell1982redshift}, which is a possible explanation for the shift in the rms profile. \\
The measured FWHM values of the Balmer rms line profiles agree with a radial stratification structure of the emitting region of the Balmer lines \parencite{korista2004optical}. While H$\alpha$ and H$\beta$ exhibits FWHM of $3100\pm 300$ and $3400 \pm 300$, H$\gamma$ and H$\delta$ exhibits broader FWHM values of $3900 \pm 400\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$ and $5900 \pm 2000\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$, respectively. \\
The FWHM of the rms profiles of show a clear ionization dependence. The higher ionized helium lines He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda1640$ and He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda4686$  show significant broader line widths than the lower ionized helium line He\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda5876$ with $6900 \pm 1000\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$, $6000 \pm 400\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$ and $4000 \pm 400\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$ for He\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda5876$, respectively. The high-ionization line doublet C\,\textsc{iv}$\,\lambda\lambda 1548,\,1550$ exhibits the broadest rms profile with $8400 \pm 500\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$, which is significantly broader compared to the lower ionized emission-lines like the Balmer lines. Therefor the measured FWHM are consisted with the inferred ionization stratification structure of the estimated characteristic BLR radii.

\subsection{Variability}

The variability of the measured continuum and broad line emission was quantified with the fractional variability, as defined in \cite{rodriguez1997steps}. The measured fractional variability of the continuum light curves follows the same trend as reported in \cite{cackett2018accretion}, with the highest variability in the UV and decreasing variability toward the NIR.\\
Similar to the time lags and line widths, the Balmer line light curves show also show a variability trend, with the highest fractional variability in H$\delta$ and the lowest in H$\alpha$, which is consistent with a radial stratification structure of the Balmer region. \\
Also, the emission-line light curves overall show higher variability for more highly ionized lines, with the exception for the He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda4686$ light curve. A possible explanation for this discrepancy may be the blend with non variable Fe\,\textsc{ii} emission interfering with the flux measurement of the line. Therefore, the observed variability trend of the emission-lines is as well consistent with an ionization stratification structure of the BLR with more highly ionized lines tend to show higher variability.



\section{Black Hole Mass}

Based on the reverberation mapping results, virial masses were derived for the selected emission-lines, and the SMBH mass was estimated using a scale factor of $f=6.9$. The adopted scale factor accounts for the low inclination angle of $i \sim 11^\circ$ reported by \cite{Ochmann_2025}. Its estimation is based on the relation $f \sim \sin^{-2}(i)$ \parencite{krolik2001systematic}, which captures the inclination dependence of the observed line-of-sight velocity dispersion. Since the velocity width is parameterized via the FWHM in this work rather than the line dispersion, an additional conversion was applied using $\sigma_{\mathrm{line}} \approx \mathrm{FWHM}/2$ \parencite{peterson2004}. Using this approach, an inverse-variance–weighted mean BH mass of $\bar{M}_{\mathrm{BH}} \approx (5.28 \pm 0.12)\times 10^{7}\,M_\odot$ was obtained from the determined values of each suitable emission-line. Earlier works reported values in the same order of magnitude, e.g. $M \approx 1.4 \times 10^7\,M_\odot$ \parencite{kollatschny1997balmer} and $M = (9.8 \pm 2.1) \times 10^6\,M_\odot$ \parencite{denney2006ngc4593}. Using a similar approach, \cite{kollatschny1997balmer} monitored H$\alpha$ and estimated the SMBH mass using a scale factor of $1.5$, whereas \cite{denney2006ngc4593} monitored H$\beta$ and used a scale factor of $5.5$. Rescaling these values to the scale factor of $6.9$ used here yields $M \approx 6.44 \times 10^7\,M_\odot$ \parencite{kollatschny1997balmer} and $M = (1.23 \pm 0.26) \times 10^7\,M_\odot$ \parencite{denney2006ngc4593}. Therefore, the SMBH mass found in this analysis is consistent in order of magnitude with these earlier estimates.

\section{Variation and Bowen Fluorescence of O\,\textsc{I}$\,\lambda8446$}
\label{sec:diskussion_BF}

The low-ionization line O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ exhibits a noticeable variability with $F_\mathrm{var} = 0.04$ in the HST/STIS dataset. Since O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ was never monitored within a reverberation-mapping campaign before, this work presents the first results of a reverberation-mapping analysis for this line. In addition to that, O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ is known to be a Bowen fluorescence line, which can be enhanced by Ly$\beta$ emission due to a near coincidental transition \parencite{grandi1980}. Due to the absence of quintet emission-lines, recombination was ruled out as a significant excitation mechanism for O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ \parencite{grandi1980}. In addition it showed, that O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ exhibits a significantly stronger correlation with Ly$\alpha$ than with UVW2. Ly$\beta$ excites O\,\textsc{i} through a near-resonant transition at $\lambda\,1025\,\AA$, followed by line transition via the O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,11287$ transition and subsequently by the O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ transition (see Figure \ref{fig:bowen_cascate}). This cascade is expected to occur with a photon ratio of 1:1 \parencite{grandi1980}. A photon ratio of $\sim 0.8$ between O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ and O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,11287$, which was taken from the near-infrared SpeX spectra obtained by \cite{landt2008near}, was determined. This result shows that Ly$\beta$ pumping is the dominant excitation mechanism in O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ in NGC\,4593. Therefore it can be assumed that the O\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda 8446$ variation in NGC\,4593 is driven through Bowen fluorescence, which enables to examine the Bowen fluorescence relation of O\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda 8446$ for the first time in a reverberation-mapping analysis. Since Ly$\beta$ is not covered in the HST/STIS dataset, Ly$\alpha$ was used as a proxy for investigation of the Bowen fluorescence O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$. Therefore, the emitting region of O\,\textsc{i} is likely located at a distance corresponding to a lag of $\sim 3.5$ days from the ionizing continuum, consistent with the Bowen fluorescence scenario, rather than the $4.7^{+2.4}_{-1.4}$ days implied by the direct lag relative to UVW2 under the assumption of recombination as the main excitation mechanism. With that, the Bowen fluorescence lag of O\,\textsc{i} corresponds to the radius of the emitting region of H$\alpha$ with $3.3 \ensuremath{_{-0.6}^{+1.1}}\,$ days. Since H$\alpha$ and O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ also exhibits a strong correlation with a minimal time shift of $\sim 0.3$ days, it can be inferred that O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ is emitted at approximately the same characteristic BLR radius as H$\alpha$ within uncertainties.


 
\section{Summary}

This thesis presents the results of the first reverberation-mapping analysis of the broad-line region (BLR) of the Seyfert I galaxy NGC\,4593 based on an HST/STIS campaign (PI: Cackett, E.M, Prop ID 14121). This dataset is ideally suited for reverberation mapping thanks to its daily cadence and its broad wavelength coverage spanning parts of the UV and the optical-to-NIR regimes. This enabled a more detailed investigation of the BLR structure than in previous RM studies of NGC\,4593 by including additional broad emission-lines that had not been covered before. The measured time lags and FWHM values are listed in Tables \ref{tab:lags_UVW2} and \ref{tab:line_width_FWHM}, respectively. The results indicate a systematic trend: higher-ionization broad emission-lines exhibit shorter time lags and broader line widths than lower-ionization lines, indicating an ionization stratified BLR \parencite{collin1988broad}.\\
Based on the virial products of the reverberation-mapped lines, a SMBH mass of $\bar{M}_{\mathrm{BH}} \approx (5.28 \pm 0.12)\times 10^{7}\,M_\odot$ was inferred, which agrees in order of magnitude with earlier estimates (e.g. \cite{kollatschny1997balmer, denney2006ngc4593}).\\
In addition, variability of the low-ionization line O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ was detected for the first time in a reverberation-mapping analysis. It was show that this variability is primarily driven through Ly$\beta$ fluorescence, making O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ the first Bowen-fluorescence line investigated with RM. Following the Bowen-fluorescence process, the emitting region was approximately found at the same distance as the emitting region of H$\alpha$ towards the ionizing continuum. The results of the analysis of the O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ variability led to the publication \parencite{ochmann2026first}.
