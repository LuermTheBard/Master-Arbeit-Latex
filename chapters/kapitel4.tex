\chapter{Discussion}

In a recent study, \cite{cackett2018accretion} conducted an observing campaign of NGC\,4593 with HST/STIS (PI: Cackett, E.M., Prop. ID 14121), covering the optical-to-NIR range between approximately $2900\,\AA$ -- $10000\,\AA$ as well as the UV range between approximately $\sim 1100\,\AA$–$1700\,\AA$. The study focused on a reverberation mapping analysis of the UV and optical continuum emission of NGC\,4593 \parencite{cackett2018accretion}. However, a reverberation mapping analysis of the BLR based on this dataset had not yet been attempted. Building on the dataset of \cite{cackett2018accretion}, this thesis presents the first reverberation mapping analysis of the BLR of NGC\,4593 based on HST/STIS observations, including broad emission lines that have not been monitored in a reverberation mapping campaign of NGC\,4593 yet. In addition, variability in the low-ionization line O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ was measured in the HST/STIS dataset (see Table \ref{tab:varstatistics_lines}), which was never monitored in a reverberation mapping campaign before. This enabled the first reverberation mapping analysis of the O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ line.  
 
\section{Structure of the BLR}
\subsection{Time Lags}
The extracted light curves of the emission lines show an overall similar shape and strong correlation with the \textit{Swift} UVW2 light curve, exhibiting a CCF maximum value between $0.7$--$0.9$. Only the O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ light curve shows a lower peak correlation with \textit{Swift} UVW2 of $0.6$, which is consistent with the exclusion of recombination as a significant excitation mechanism for O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ (see Section \ref{sec:BF_Analysis}).\\
H$\alpha$ and H$\beta$ show time lags of $3.3^{+1.1}_{-0.6}\,$ and $2.3^{+1.0}_{-0.3}\,$days which agrees with the results of earlier works \parencite{kollatschny1997balmer, denney2006ngc4593, Williams_2018}. The higher-order Balmer lines H$\gamma$ and H$\delta$ show a significantly shorter time lag with $1.7^{+0.2}_{-0.4}\,$days and $1.7^{+0.4}_{-0.4}\,$days, respectively. This implies a radially stratified structure of the Balmer-line emitting region, where higher-order lines respond more rapidly to continuum variations than lower-order lines. A possible explanation for this stratification are optical depth effects within the Balmer-line emitting region \parencite{korista2004optical}. \\
The time lags among the helium lines exhibit a systematic difference, depending on their ionization level. He\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda5876$ lags $3.3^{+0.7}_{-0.4}\,$days behind \textit{Swift} UVW2, while the higher-ionized He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda1640$ and He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda4686$ show a much faster response time with lags of $0.3^{+0.6}_{-0.2}\,$days and $0.7^{+0.1}_{-0.3}\,$days, respectively. Therefore, the time lag of He\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda5876$ is consistent with the lag of H$\alpha$, while the He\,\textsc{ii} lines exhibit significantly shorter delays.\\
The high-ionization UV lines all show consistently short lags of approximately $\sim 1$\, day, with values of $1.0 \ensuremath{_{-0.4}^{+0.2}}$, $1.2 \ensuremath{_{-0.4}^{+0.1}}$ and $0.8 \ensuremath{_{-0.1}^{+0.5}}$ days for N\,\textsc{v}$\,\lambda\lambda 1238,\,1242$, Si\,\textsc{iv} $\,\lambda\lambda 1393,\,1402$ and C\,\textsc{iv}$\,\lambda\lambda 1548,\,1550$, respectively.\\ The ionization dependency seen for the helium line time lags and the consistent smaller time lags of high-ionization lines compared to the lower ionized Balmer lines implies an ionization stratification structure of the BLR, which is consistent with BLR structures reported for other Seyfert galaxies (e.g. \cite{collin1988broad, gaskell1986line, kollatschny2001short}).\\
Since the UVW2 band lags behind the extracted HST/STIS $1150\,\AA$ continuum by approximately $0.5\,$days, this offset was added for the final estimation of the characteristic BLR radii for each emission line (see Table \ref{tab:lags_UVW2}).



\subsection{Line Profiles}
Based on the assumption that the BLR is dominated by the gravitational potential of the SMBH, the velocity dispersion of the BLR can be parameterized by the line widths of the rms profiles of the broad emission lines \parencite{peterson2004}, as done in earlier reverberation mapping studies (e.g. \cite{kollatschny1997balmer, denney2006ngc4593}).\\
The Balmer rms line profiles show a similar asymmetric, double-peaked structure and are shifted toward positive velocities. This shift is also noticeable in the Ly$\alpha$ profile and He\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda5876$ profile. This indicates that most of the line variability of these lines originates from the receding part of their emitting regions.\\
In contrast, the maxima of He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda1640$ and He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda4686$ rms profiles show no velocity shift compared to their mean profiles. A similar behavior is observed in the rms profiles of the high-ionization lines N\,\textsc{v}$\,\lambda\lambda 1238,\,1242$, Si\,\textsc{iv}$\,\lambda\lambda 1393,\,1402$ and C\,\textsc{iv}$\,\lambda\lambda 1548,\,1550$. High-ionized broad emission lines often show a blue-shift compared to lower-ionized broad emission lines \parencite{gaskell1982redshift}, which is a possible explanation for the shift in the rms profile. \\
While H$\alpha$ and H$\beta$ exhibit FWHM of $(3100\pm 300)\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$ and $(3400 \pm 300)\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$ respectively, H$\gamma$ and H$\delta$ exhibit broader FWHM values of $(3900 \pm 400)\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$ and $(5900 \pm 2000)\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$, respectively, which agrees with a radially stratified structure of the emitting region of the Balmer lines \parencite{korista2004optical}. \\
Similar to the time lags, the FWHM of the helium rms profiles imply a clear ionization dependence. While the lower ionized helium line He\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda5876$ shows a line width of $(4000 \pm 400)\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$, the higher ionized helium lines He\,\textsc{ii}\,$\lambda1640$ and He\,\textsc{ii}\,$\lambda4686$ exhibit significantly broader line widths of $(6900 \pm 1500)\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$ and $(6000 \pm 400)\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$, respectively. The high-ionization line doublet C\,\textsc{iv}$\,\lambda\lambda 1548,\,1550$ exhibits the broadest rms profile of the monitored lines, with an FWHM of $(8400 \pm 1000)\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$. Therefore, the FWHM of the rms line profiles are consistent with the ionization stratification structure of the BLR inferred by the estimated characteristic BLR radii.

\subsection{Variability}

The variability of the continuum and broad emission lines was quantified with the maximum-to-minimum ratio and fractional variability of the light curves, as defined in \cite{rodriguez1997steps}. The variability of the continuum light curves follows the same trend as reported in \cite{cackett2018accretion}, with the highest variability in the UV and decreasing variability toward the NIR.\\
The Balmer line light curves follow an increasing variability trend for higher orders, with the highest fractional variability in H$\delta$ and the lowest in H$\alpha$. Furthermore, the variability of the emission line light curves suggests an ionization dependence, as seen for the time lags and line widths, with higher variability measured for higher-ionized lines, with the exception of the He\,\textsc{ii}$\,\lambda4686$ light curve. A possible explanation for this discrepancy may be the blend with the non-variable Fe\,\textsc{ii} band emission (see Figure \ref{fig:line_profiles}), which interferes with the flux measurement of the line. 



\section{Black Hole Mass}

Based on the reverberation mapping results, virial masses were derived for the selected emission lines, and the SMBH mass was estimated using a scale factor of $f=6.9$. The adopted scale factor accounts for the low inclination angle of $i \sim 11^\circ$ reported by \cite{Ochmann_2025}. It is based on the relation $f \sim \sin^{-2}(i)$ \parencite{krolik2001systematic}, which captures the inclination dependence of the observed line-of-sight velocity dispersion, and on the relation $\sigma_{\mathrm{line}} \approx \mathrm{FWHM}/2$ \parencite{peterson2004}, since the velocity dispersion is parameterized with the FWHM of the rms line profiles. Using this approach, an inverse-variance–weighted mean SMBH mass of $\bar{M}_{\mathrm{BH}} \approx (5.28 \pm 0.12)\times 10^{7}\,M_\odot$ was obtained. Earlier works reported values on the order of magnitude of $10^7\,M_\odot$ for NGC\,4593 (e.g., \cite{kollatschny1997balmer, denney2006ngc4593}). Using a similar approach, \cite{kollatschny1997balmer} monitored H$\alpha$ and estimated the SMBH mass using a scale factor of $1.5$, whereas \cite{denney2006ngc4593} monitored H$\beta$ and used a scale factor of $5.5$. Rescaling these values to the scale factor of $6.9$ used here yields $M \approx 6.44 \times 10^7\,M_\odot$ \parencite{kollatschny1997balmer} and $M = (1.23 \pm 0.26) \times 10^7\,M_\odot$ \parencite{denney2006ngc4593}. Therefore, the estimated SMBH mass of this work is consistent in order of magnitude with these earlier estimates.

\section{Variation and Bowen Fluorescence of O\,\textsc{I}$\,\lambda8446$}
\label{sec:discussion_BF}

The low-ionization line O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ shows variability with $F_\mathrm{var} = 0.04$ in the HST/STIS dataset. As O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ has not previously been included in a reverberation mapping campaign, this work presents the first reverberation mapping analysis of this low-ionization line. Furthermore, O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ is known to be a Bowen fluorescence line, which can be enhanced by Ly$\beta$ emission due to a near-coincidental transition \parencite{grandi1980}. Due to the absence of quintet emission lines in the HST/STIS dataset, recombination can be excluded as a significant excitation mechanism for O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ \parencite{grandi1980}. Ly$\beta$ excites O\,\textsc{i} through the near-coincidental transition at $\lambda\,1025\,\AA$, followed by line transition via the O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,11287$ transition and subsequently by the O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ transition (see Figure \ref{fig:bowen_cascate}). This cascade is expected to occur with a photon ratio of 1:1 \parencite{grandi1980}. Since O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,11287$ is not covered in the HST/STIS dataset, its flux is measured in the NIR SpeX spectra presented by \cite{landt2008near}. From this, a photon ratio of $\sim 0.8$ between O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ and O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,11287$ is derived, which is close to unity. Together with the exclusion of recombination established above, this suggests that Ly$\beta$ pumping is the dominant excitation mechanism of O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ in NGC\,4593. This enables, for the first time, a reverberation mapping analysis of the Bowen fluorescence process of O\,\textsc{i}$\,\lambda 8446$. Since Ly$\beta$ is not covered in the HST/STIS dataset, Ly$\alpha$ is used as a proxy to investigate the Bowen fluorescence of O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$. By combining the delay between the light curves of Ly$\alpha$ and UVW2 with that between O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ and Ly$\alpha$, a lag of $\sim 3.5\,$days is estimated for O\,\textsc{i} under Bowen fluorescence, rather than $4.7^{+2.4}_{-1.4}\,$days expected for recombination. The resulting Bowen fluorescence lag of O\,\textsc{i} is thus consistent with the lag of H$\alpha$ of $3.3 \ensuremath{_{-0.6}^{+1.1}}\,$ days. Since H$\alpha$ and O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ also exhibit a strong correlation with a negligible time delay of $\sim 0.3$ days within uncertainty, it can be inferred that O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ originates from approximately the same characteristic BLR radius as H$\alpha$.


 
\section{Summary}

This thesis presents the results of the first reverberation mapping analysis of the broad line region (BLR) of the Seyfert 1 galaxy NGC\,4593 based on an HST/STIS campaign (PI: Cackett, E.M., Prop. ID 14121). This dataset is ideally suited for reverberation mapping thanks to its daily cadence and broad wavelength coverage spanning parts of the UV and the optical-to-NIR regimes. This enabled a more detailed investigation of the BLR structure than in previous reverberation mapping studies of NGC\,4593 by including broad emission lines that had not been monitored before. The measured time lags and FWHM values are listed in Tables \ref{tab:lags_UVW2} and \ref{tab:line_width_FWHM}, respectively. The results indicate a systematic trend: higher-ionization broad emission lines exhibit shorter time lags and broader line widths than lower-ionization lines, indicating an ionization stratification structure of the BLR \parencite{collin1988broad}.\\
Based on the virial products of the reverberation-mapped lines, an SMBH mass of $\bar{M}_{\mathrm{BH}} \approx (5.28 \pm 0.12)\times 10^{7}\,M_\odot$ was inferred, which agrees in order of magnitude with earlier estimates (e.g. \cite{kollatschny1997balmer, denney2006ngc4593}).\\
In addition, variability of the low-ionization line O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ was detected for the first time in a reverberation mapping analysis. It was shown that this variability is primarily driven by Ly$\beta$ fluorescence, making O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ the first Bowen-fluorescence line investigated with reverberation mapping. Consistent with the Bowen fluorescence process, the emitting region of O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ was found to be located at approximately the same characteristic BLR radius as the emitting region of H$\alpha$. The results of the O\,\textsc{i}\,$\lambda\,8446$ variability analysis further led to the publication \parencite{ochmann2026first}.
